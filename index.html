About

I am a PhD researcher at UCLA studying ice–ocean interactions in Antarctica. I focus on submesoscale processes beneath ice shelves that are often overlooked in regional and climate models. My work combines numerical modeling, theory, and observational insights to understand these complex systems. I also enjoy mentoring undergraduates and exploring ways to make science approachable and engaging.

Research Projects

Sea-Ice – Ocean Interactions

Using numerical models and observational data to study ice–ocean interactions in Antarctica. Focus on understanding processes that influence regional circulation and sub-ice shelf dynamics.

Sub-Ice Shelf Submesoscale Dynamics

Investigating small-scale turbulent and submesoscale processes beneath ice shelves that are not resolved in current climate models, with potential implications for ice shelf stability and ocean mixing.

DIYnamics / Volunteer Work

Active volunteer in the DIYnamics community, promoting science outreach and education. Lead workshops and hands-on experiments to engage students in fluid dynamics.
